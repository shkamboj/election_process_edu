# Philippines Election Process

## Constitutional Framework and System of Government

The Philippines is a unitary presidential constitutional republic governed by the 1987 Constitution. The system features separation of powers among the executive (President), legislative (Congress), and judicial branches. The President serves as head of state and government for a single six-year term with no re-election. The Vice President is elected separately. Congress is bicameral: the Senate (24 seats, elected nationally) and the House of Representatives (approximately 316 seats: 253 district representatives + party-list representatives comprising 20% of total seats).

## Election Administration Body

The Commission on Elections (COMELEC) is the independent constitutional body that administers all elections. COMELEC has seven members appointed by the President with consent of the Commission on Appointments. It registers voters and political parties, adjudicates election disputes (except for President and Vice President), and manages the automated election system. Website: www.comelec.gov.ph

## Types of Elections

- **National elections**: President, Vice President, 12 Senators (half of Senate every 3 years), House district and party-list representatives
- **Local elections**: Governors, vice governors, provincial board members, mayors, vice mayors, city/municipal councilors, barangay officials
- **National and local elections are synchronized**: Held every three years (midterm and presidential alternate)
- **Barangay and Sangguniang Kabataan elections**: Village-level elections
- **Plebiscites and referendums**: As needed for constitutional amendments or local matters

## Voter Registration Process

All Filipino citizens aged 18 and above on election day are eligible to vote. Registration is conducted by COMELEC through biometric voter registration using fingerprints, photographs, and signatures. Voters are registered in their place of residence. Registration periods are set by COMELEC, typically closing several months before election day. Deactivated voters (those who failed to vote in two consecutive elections) must reactivate. Overseas Filipino Workers can register for overseas absentee voting.

## Election Timeline/Calendar

- **Voter registration deadline**: Approximately 6 months before election
- **Certificate of candidacy filing**: October of the year before election
- **Campaign period**: 90 days for national candidates, 45 days for local candidates (before election day)
- **Election day**: Second Monday of May every three years
- **Canvassing of results**: Days to weeks after election
- **Inauguration**: June 30 (President and Vice President); other officials per law

## Voting Methods and Technology

The Philippines uses an Automated Election System (AES) since 2010. Voters shade ovals on pre-printed paper ballots, which are fed into Vote Counting Machines (VCMs/optical mark readers) at precinct level. The VCM scans, counts, and stores digital ballot images. Results are transmitted electronically to the COMELEC transparency server and canvassing centers. Paper ballots are retained for audit and recount purposes. Random manual audits verify machine accuracy.

## Campaign Rules and Finance

Campaign spending limits are set per voter in the candidate's constituency (₱5 per voter for President and Vice President, ₱3 per voter for other positions, adjusted periodically). Candidates must file Statements of Contributions and Expenditures (SOCE) within 30 days after election. Premature campaigning rules apply before the official campaign period. Equal access to media time is encouraged. Vote-buying and partisan use of public funds are prohibited. The Fair Elections Act regulates campaign advertising.

## Counting and Results Process

VCMs count ballots at each precinct and produce an Election Returns document. Results are transmitted electronically and also physically delivered to canvassing boards. The Board of Canvassers at municipal, provincial, and national levels canvasses results progressively. Congress (sitting as the National Board of Canvassers) canvasses votes for President and Vice President. The Supreme Court, sitting as the Presidential Electoral Tribunal, resolves presidential/VP election protests.

## Post-Election Procedures

COMELEC proclaims winners for senatorial and party-list races. Congress proclaims the President and Vice President after canvassing. Election protests for President and Vice President are filed with the Supreme Court (Presidential Electoral Tribunal). Other protests go to COMELEC or courts. The transition period runs from proclamation to inauguration. Inauguration is June 30 for the President and Vice President. COMELEC conducts post-election audits and publishes reports.

## Key Legislation

- 1987 Constitution of the Republic of the Philippines
- Omnibus Election Code (Batas Pambansa Blg. 881)
- Republic Act No. 9369 (Automated Election System Law, as amended)
- Republic Act No. 7166 (Synchronized Elections Act)
- Republic Act No. 9189 (Overseas Absentee Voting Act, as amended)
- Republic Act No. 7941 (Party-List System Act)
- Fair Elections Act (Republic Act No. 9006)
- COMELEC Resolutions and Rules of Procedure
