# Types of Elections in India

## 1. Lok Sabha Elections (General Elections)
- **What**: Election to the lower house of Parliament
- **Seats**: 543 constituencies across India
- **Who votes**: All registered voters in each constituency
- **System**: First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) — candidate with the most votes wins
- **Term**: 5 years (can be dissolved earlier)
- **Conducted by**: Election Commission of India
- **Frequency**: Every 5 years (or earlier if Lok Sabha is dissolved)
- **Phases**: Usually conducted in multiple phases (7 phases in 2024) due to the massive scale
- **Significance**: The party/coalition that wins a majority (272+ seats) forms the Central Government; their leader becomes the Prime Minister

## 2. Rajya Sabha Elections
- **What**: Election to the upper house of Parliament
- **Seats**: 245 total (233 elected + 12 nominated by President)
- **Who votes**: Elected members of State Legislative Assemblies and Union Territory assemblies (indirect election)
- **System**: Single Transferable Vote (STV) with proportional representation
- **Term**: 6 years; one-third of members retire every 2 years (biennial elections)
- **Note**: Rajya Sabha is a permanent body — it cannot be dissolved

## 3. State Legislative Assembly Elections (Vidhan Sabha)
- **What**: Election to the state legislature's lower/only house
- **Seats**: Varies by state (e.g., UP: 403, Goa: 40, Sikkim: 32)
- **Who votes**: All registered voters in the state's constituencies
- **System**: FPTP
- **Term**: 5 years (can be dissolved earlier under President's Rule)
- **Conducted by**: Election Commission of India
- **Significance**: Winning party/coalition forms the State Government; leader becomes Chief Minister

## 4. State Legislative Council Elections (Vidhan Parishad)
- **What**: Election to the upper house of state legislature (only 6 states have this: Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh)
- **Members elected by**: MLAs, local body members, teachers, graduates, and Governor's nominees
- **System**: STV with proportional representation
- **Term**: 6 years; one-third retire every 2 years

## 5. Presidential Election
- **What**: Election of the President of India
- **Electoral College**: Elected members of both houses of Parliament + elected members of all State Legislative Assemblies + elected members of Delhi and Puducherry assemblies
- **System**: STV with proportional representation; value of votes varies by state population
- **Term**: 5 years
- **Frequency**: Every 5 years

## 6. Vice-Presidential Election
- **What**: Election of the Vice-President of India
- **Electoral College**: Members of both houses of Parliament (elected + nominated)
- **System**: STV
- **Term**: 5 years

## 7. Local Body Elections (Panchayat & Municipal)
- **What**: Elections to local self-government bodies
- **Rural**: Gram Panchayat → Panchayat Samiti (Block) → Zila Parishad (District)
- **Urban**: Municipal Corporation / Municipal Council / Nagar Panchayat
- **Conducted by**: State Election Commission (separate from ECI)
- **Term**: 5 years
- **Reservation**: Seats reserved for SC, ST, OBC, and Women (minimum 33% for women, 50% in some states)
- **Governed by**: 73rd Amendment (Panchayats) and 74th Amendment (Municipalities)

## 8. By-Elections
- **What**: Election held to fill a vacancy caused by death, resignation, or disqualification of a sitting member
- **Scope**: Single constituency
- **Must be held**: Within 6 months of the vacancy (unless the remaining term is less than 1 year)
- **Same rules**: Same process as the original election for that seat
