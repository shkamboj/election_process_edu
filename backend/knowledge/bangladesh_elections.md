# Bangladesh Election Process

## Constitutional Framework and System of Government

Bangladesh is a unitary parliamentary republic governed by the 1972 Constitution. The system follows a Westminster-style parliamentary model where the Prime Minister is the head of government and the President is a ceremonial head of state elected by Parliament. The unicameral Parliament (Jatiya Sangsad) has 300 directly elected seats plus 50 seats reserved for women (elected by parliamentarians). The Prime Minister is the leader of the majority party and exercises executive authority.

## Election Administration Body

The Bangladesh Election Commission (BEC) is an independent constitutional body responsible for conducting all elections, including parliamentary, presidential, local government, and referendums. The Commission consists of a Chief Election Commissioner and up to four Election Commissioners appointed by the President. The BEC prepares electoral rolls, conducts elections, and resolves registration disputes. Website: www.ecs.gov.bd

## Types of Elections

- **National parliamentary elections**: 300 single-member constituencies (first-past-the-post)
- **Presidential election**: Elected by members of Parliament
- **Local government elections**: Union Parishad, Upazila Parishad, Zila Parishad, Pourashavas (municipalities), and City Corporations
- **By-elections**: To fill vacancies in Parliament
- Parliamentary elections held every five years

## Voter Registration Process

All Bangladeshi citizens aged 18 and above are eligible to vote. The BEC maintains a National Voter Register using a digital photograph-based voter list with National ID cards. Voter registration is conducted door-to-door and at registration centers. Each voter receives a National ID card with photograph, which serves as the voter identification document. The electoral roll is updated annually and displayed for claims and objections.

## Election Timeline/Calendar

- **Dissolution of Parliament**: At the end of five-year term or earlier
- **Election announcement**: Caretaker/interim period begins (historically under Caretaker Government system, now modified)
- **Nomination filing**: Within specified period after announcement
- **Scrutiny of nominations**: 3-5 days after filing deadline
- **Campaign period**: Typically 2-3 weeks
- **Election day**: Within 90 days of dissolution
- **Results announcement**: Within days of polling

## Voting Methods and Technology

Bangladesh uses paper ballots marked with a stamp bearing the official seal. Voters stamp next to the symbol of their chosen candidate. Transparent ballot boxes are used at polling stations. Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) have been piloted in select constituencies. Voter identification is done through National ID cards with photograph verification. Polling stations are staffed by Presiding Officers and polling assistants with security provided by law enforcement.

## Campaign Rules and Finance

Political parties must register with the BEC. Campaign spending limits are set for each constituency. Use of religion for electoral purposes is prohibited. Campaign activities must cease 24 hours before polling. Government resources cannot be used for campaigning. The BEC issues a Code of Conduct for elections. Parties must submit election expenditure statements after elections. Media coverage rules apply during the campaign period.

## Counting and Results Process

Ballot boxes are opened and votes counted at polling centers in the presence of candidate agents and observers. Presiding Officers record results and send them to the Returning Officer. Results are announced at the constituency level by Returning Officers. The BEC consolidates and publishes national results within days. Unofficial results are often available on election night through media reporting.

## Post-Election Procedures

The BEC formally declares election results and gazettes the names of elected members. The President summons the new Parliament within 30 days of results. The leader of the majority party is invited to form the government. Election disputes are filed as election petitions in the High Court Division within 30 days. Post-election reviews are conducted by the BEC and civil society organizations.

## Key Legislation

- Constitution of the People's Republic of Bangladesh (1972, as amended)
- Representation of the People Order, 1972
- Electoral Rolls Ordinance, 1982
- Political Parties Registration Rules
- Bangladesh Election Commission Rules
- Code of Conduct for Political Parties and Candidates
- Local Government Acts (Union Parishad, Upazila, City Corporation)
