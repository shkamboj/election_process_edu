# Election Timeline — Step by Step

## Overview
Indian elections follow a structured timeline mandated by law and managed by the Election Commission of India (ECI). Here's the step-by-step process from announcement to government formation.

## Phase 1: Pre-Election

### Step 1: Election Announcement
- The ECI announces the election schedule at a **press conference**
- Announces: dates for nomination, scrutiny, withdrawal, polling (phase-wise), and counting
- **Model Code of Conduct (MCC)** comes into effect immediately from the date of announcement
- For Lok Sabha, elections must be held before the term of the current Lok Sabha expires

### Step 2: Notification
- The President (for Lok Sabha) or Governor (for Vidhan Sabha) issues a formal **notification** calling upon constituencies to elect members
- Typically issued **7 days** before the last date for filing nominations
- This formally begins the election process

## Phase 2: Nomination

### Step 3: Filing of Nominations
- Candidates file their **nomination papers** with the Returning Officer (RO) of their constituency
- Must include:
  - Personal details and affidavit (criminal record, assets, liabilities, educational qualifications)
  - **Security deposit**: ₹25,000 for general candidates, ₹12,500 for SC/ST candidates (Lok Sabha); ₹10,000 and ₹5,000 for state assemblies
  - Party ticket letter (for party candidates) or declaration as independent
- Nomination period: Usually **7+ days** from the date of notification
- A candidate can file **up to 4 nomination papers** for the same constituency

### Step 4: Scrutiny of Nominations
- The RO examines all nomination papers on the **day after the last date** of filing
- Checks: eligibility (age, citizenship), proper signatures, security deposit, valid proposer
- Invalid nominations are **rejected** with reasons recorded
- Candidates can be present during scrutiny

### Step 5: Withdrawal of Candidature
- Candidates can withdraw their nomination **within 2 days** after scrutiny
- After withdrawal deadline, the list of **contesting candidates** is finalized
- If only one valid candidate remains, they are declared **elected unopposed**

## Phase 3: Campaigning

### Step 6: Election Campaign
- The campaign period runs from nomination until **48 hours before polling day** (the "silence period")
- Activities include: rallies, door-to-door canvassing, media advertisements, social media campaigns
- **Model Code of Conduct** restricts:
  - Use of government machinery for campaigning
  - Communal or divisive speeches
  - Distribution of liquor, money, or gifts to voters
  - New policy announcements by the ruling party
- ECI monitors through flying squads, surveillance teams, and the cVIGIL app

### Step 7: Silence Period
- **48 hours before polling**, all campaigning must stop
- No rallies, no advertisements, no canvassing
- Meant to give voters time to reflect and make a free choice
- Media can report but cannot broadcast opinions or exit polls

## Phase 4: Polling

### Step 8: Polling Day
- Voting takes place at designated polling stations from **7:00 AM to 6:00 PM** (may vary)
- **Multi-phase polling**: In large states, polling is spread across multiple days/phases to allow security force deployment
  - Example: 2024 Lok Sabha elections had **7 phases** over 44 days
- Voters:
  1. Go to their assigned polling station
  2. Show valid photo ID
  3. Get their finger marked with **indelible ink**
  4. Receive a ballot slip (in some cases)
  5. Enter the voting compartment
  6. Press the button next to their chosen candidate on the **EVM**
  7. Verify their vote on the **VVPAT** slip (visible for 7 seconds)
  8. Exit the polling station

### Step 9: Closing and Sealing
- At the end of polling hours, EVMs are **sealed** in the presence of polling agents
- Sealed EVMs are transported to **strong rooms** (secure storage) under security escort
- Strong rooms are guarded 24/7 with CCTV surveillance until counting day
- Candidates' agents can be present during sealing and transport

## Phase 5: Counting and Results

### Step 10: Counting of Votes
- Counting takes place on the **designated counting day** (usually 2-3 days after the last phase)
- Counting is done at **counting centers** under the supervision of the RO
- Process:
  1. **Postal ballots** counted first
  2. **EVM votes** counted round by round (each round = votes from a set of EVMs)
  3. Results tallied on counting boards visible to counting agents
  4. **VVPAT verification**: 5 randomly selected polling stations per constituency — paper slips matched with EVM count
- Candidates' counting agents are present throughout
- Trends and results are updated on the **ECI results website** in real-time

### Step 11: Declaration of Results
- Once counting is complete, the RO declares the **winning candidate**
- The candidate receives a **Certificate of Election**
- Results are published in the Official Gazette
- Total results for all constituencies are compiled by ECI

## Phase 6: Government Formation

### Step 12: Government Formation
- **Lok Sabha**: The party (or coalition) with a majority (272+ seats) is invited by the President to form the government
- The leader of the majority party/coalition is appointed **Prime Minister**
- The PM recommends a Council of Ministers, sworn in by the President
- **State Assembly**: The party/coalition with a majority is invited by the Governor; leader becomes **Chief Minister**
- If no party has a clear majority, the Governor/President may invite the single largest party or coalition to prove majority on the floor of the house

### Step 13: First Session and Oath
- Newly elected members take the **oath of office** in the first session of the new house
- A **Speaker** is elected for Lok Sabha / State Assembly
- The government presents its agenda

## Typical Timeline Summary (Lok Sabha)

| Event | Typical Duration |
|-------|-----------------|
| Announcement to first phase polling | ~3-4 weeks |
| Nomination period | ~1 week |
| Scrutiny | 1 day after nominations close |
| Withdrawal | 2 days after scrutiny |
| Campaign period | ~2-3 weeks |
| Silence period | 48 hours before each phase |
| Polling (all phases) | 4-7 weeks |
| Counting | 1 day (usually 2-3 days after last phase) |
| Government formation | Within days of results |
| **Total process** | **~6-8 weeks** |
