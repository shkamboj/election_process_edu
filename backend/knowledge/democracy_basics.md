# Indian Democracy — Basics

## Overview
India is the world's largest democracy with over 960 million eligible voters (as of 2024). It is a **sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic** governed by the Constitution of India, adopted on 26 January 1950.

## Parliamentary System
India follows a **Westminster-style parliamentary democracy** with two houses:
- **Lok Sabha (House of the People)**: Lower house, 543 elected members + 2 nominated Anglo-Indian members (removed by 104th Amendment, 2020). Members elected directly by the people for a 5-year term.
- **Rajya Sabha (Council of States)**: Upper house, 245 members — 233 elected by state/UT legislatures, 12 nominated by the President. Members serve 6-year terms, with one-third retiring every 2 years.

## Constitutional Provisions for Elections
- **Part XV (Articles 324–329)** of the Constitution deals with elections.
- **Article 324**: Superintendence, direction, and control of elections vested in the Election Commission of India.
- **Article 325**: No person shall be ineligible for inclusion in the electoral roll on grounds of religion, race, caste, or sex.
- **Article 326**: Elections to Lok Sabha and State Assemblies shall be on the basis of **universal adult suffrage** (every citizen 18 years or older).
- **Article 327**: Parliament may make provisions regarding elections to Lok Sabha and State Legislatures.
- **Article 328**: State Legislature may make provisions regarding elections to the State Legislature.
- **Article 329**: Courts shall not interfere in electoral matters except through an election petition.

## Fundamental Principles
1. **Universal Adult Suffrage**: Every citizen aged 18+ has the right to vote regardless of caste, religion, gender, or economic status. The voting age was lowered from 21 to 18 by the **61st Amendment Act, 1988**.
2. **One Person, One Vote**: Each voter has one vote of equal value.
3. **Secret Ballot**: Voting is conducted by secret ballot to protect voter privacy.
4. **Free and Fair Elections**: The Election Commission ensures elections are conducted impartially.
5. **Regular Elections**: Elections must be held at regular intervals (every 5 years for Lok Sabha and State Assemblies).

## Federal Structure and Elections
- **Central Level**: Lok Sabha (Parliament) and Rajya Sabha elections
- **State Level**: Vidhan Sabha (State Legislative Assembly) and Vidhan Parishad (State Legislative Council, where it exists)
- **Local Level**: Panchayati Raj Institutions (rural) and Municipalities (urban) — governed by the 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments

## Key Facts
- First General Election: 1951–52 (over 4 months, 173 million voters)
- Current number of Lok Sabha constituencies: 543
- Largest constituency by area: Ladakh
- Largest constituency by voters: Malkajgiri, Telangana
- India uses **First-Past-The-Post (FPTP)** system for Lok Sabha and Vidhan Sabha elections
