# Nigeria Election Process

## Constitutional Framework and System of Government

Nigeria is a federal presidential constitutional republic governed by the 1999 Constitution (as amended). The system features a strong presidency with separation of powers among executive, legislative, and judicial branches. The President serves as head of state and government for a four-year term, renewable once. The National Assembly is bicameral: the Senate (109 seats, 3 per state plus 1 for FCT) and the House of Representatives (360 seats). Nigeria has 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory (Abuja).

## Election Administration Body

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) is responsible for conducting all federal elections, including presidential, National Assembly, and governorship elections. State Independent Electoral Commissions (SIECs) handle local government elections. INEC manages voter registration, party registration, candidate nomination, and result declaration. Website: www.inecnigeria.org

## Types of Elections

- **Presidential election**: Direct popular vote with a distribution requirement (25% in at least 2/3 of states)
- **National Assembly elections**: Senate and House of Representatives (first-past-the-post)
- **Governorship elections**: Direct election in each state (held two weeks after presidential election)
- **State House of Assembly elections**: Held simultaneously with governorship elections
- **Local government elections**: Conducted by SIECs
- General elections occur every four years

## Voter Registration Process

All Nigerian citizens aged 18 and above are eligible to vote. INEC conducts Continuous Voter Registration (CVR) using biometric data capture (fingerprints and facial recognition). Voters receive a Permanent Voter Card (PVC) containing biometric data and a chip. The PVC is required for voter authentication on election day. Registration is done at INEC offices and designated centers. The voter register is displayed for claims and objections before elections.

## Election Timeline/Calendar

- **Voter registration**: Continuous, with cutoff before elections
- **Notice of election**: At least 360 days before election
- **Party primaries**: 6-8 months before election
- **Campaign period**: 150 days before election until 24 hours before polling
- **Presidential/NASS elections**: Typically held in February
- **Governorship/State Assembly**: Two weeks after presidential election
- **Inauguration**: May 29

## Voting Methods and Technology

Nigeria uses paper ballots with the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) for voter verification. BVAS uses fingerprint and facial recognition to authenticate voters. After accreditation, voters mark ballots and deposit them in ballot boxes. INEC introduced the INEC Result Viewing Portal (IReV) for real-time upload of polling unit results. Voting takes place at open-air polling units, typically schools and public spaces. Modified open ballot system allows voters to queue behind their chosen candidate's agent in some elections.

## Campaign Rules and Finance

Political parties must register with INEC. Campaign spending limits are set by the Electoral Act (₦15 billion for presidential candidates). Campaigns must be issue-based; ethnic or religious incitement is prohibited. Campaign finance reports must be filed with INEC. Parties must give 21 days' notice before public rallies. Government resources cannot be used for campaigning. A 24-hour moratorium on campaigning precedes election day.

## Counting and Results Process

Votes are counted openly at each polling unit immediately after polls close. Results are recorded on Form EC8A and uploaded to the IReV portal. Results are collated progressively at ward, local government, state, and national levels. Collation Officers announce results at each level. The Chairman of INEC declares the final presidential election results. The process typically takes 2-5 days for national results.

## Post-Election Procedures

Election petitions must be filed within 21 days of result declaration. Election Petition Tribunals hear cases within 180 days. Appeals go to the Court of Appeal and ultimately the Supreme Court. The President is inaugurated on May 29. Gubernatorial inaugurations also occur on May 29. INEC publishes final reports and conducts post-election reviews.

## Key Legislation

- Electoral Act, 2022
- INEC Regulations and Guidelines for Elections
- Political Parties Registration and Regulation
