# The Voting Process — A Voter's Complete Guide

## Before Polling Day

### Check Your Registration
1. Visit **https://voters.eci.gov.in** (NVSP Portal)
2. Click "Search in Electoral Roll"
3. Search by **EPIC number** or by **name and address**
4. Verify your details: name, address, polling station, part number, serial number
5. Alternatively, use the **Voter Helpline App** or call **1950**

### Find Your Polling Station
- Your polling station is assigned based on your address in the electoral roll
- Check on NVSP portal or Voter Helpline app
- Polling station details are printed on your voter slip (if distributed before polling day)
- Each polling station typically covers **800-1200 voters**

### Documents to Carry
You need **one** of the following 12 approved photo IDs:
1. EPIC (Voter ID Card) / e-EPIC
2. Aadhaar Card
3. Passport
4. Driving License
5. PAN Card
6. Service ID (government/PSU employees)
7. MNREGA Job Card
8. Health Insurance Smart Card (under RSBY)
9. Bank/Post Office Passbook with photo
10. Smart Card issued by RGI (NPR)
11. Pension document with photo
12. MP/MLA/MLC identity card

## On Polling Day

### Step-by-Step Voting Process

**Step 1: Arrive at the Polling Station**
- Polling hours: Usually **7:00 AM to 6:00 PM** (may vary by state/season)
- Anyone in the queue before closing time **will be allowed to vote**
- Look for the queue — there may be separate lines for men, women, and senior citizens/PwD

**Step 2: Queue and Identification**
- Wait in the queue for your turn
- When you reach the polling officer's table, show your **photo ID**
- The officer verifies your name in the marked copy of the **electoral roll**
- Your identity is confirmed

**Step 3: Indelible Ink Marking**
- The polling officer marks your **left index finger** with indelible ink
- The ink contains **silver nitrate** and lasts approximately 4 weeks
- This prevents double voting
- The officer also checks your finger for existing ink marks

**Step 4: Receive Voter Slip**
- You receive a **voter slip** with your details and serial number
- Sign or put your thumb impression on the register

**Step 5: Enter the Voting Compartment**
- Proceed to the **screened voting compartment** for privacy
- Inside you'll find the **Ballot Unit** (part of the EVM) and the **VVPAT** machine

**Step 6: Cast Your Vote**
- The Ballot Unit displays candidate names, party symbols, and serial numbers
- **Press the blue button** next to your chosen candidate
- You'll hear a **beep** and see a **red light** next to the selected candidate — this confirms your vote
- **NOTA option**: The last button on the Ballot Unit — "None of the Above"

**Step 7: Verify on VVPAT**
- After pressing the button, look at the **VVPAT window**
- A printed slip showing the **candidate name, serial number, and party symbol** is displayed for **7 seconds**
- The slip then drops into a sealed box
- If the slip shows the wrong candidate, immediately inform the Presiding Officer

**Step 8: Exit**
- Leave the voting compartment
- Exit the polling station
- **Do not discuss** whom you voted for near the polling station

## Special Voting Provisions

### Postal Ballot / ETPBS
**Electronically Transmitted Postal Ballot System (ETPBS):**
- Ballot is sent electronically to the voter
- Voter marks the ballot, puts it in a sealed envelope, and sends it back by post
- Available for:
  - **Service voters** (armed forces, diplomatic staff)
  - **Government employees on election duty**
  - **Senior citizens (80+)** and **PwD (40%+)** — optional
  - **Voters under preventive detention**
  - **Essential service workers** on duty on polling day

### Proxy Voting
- Currently available only for **classified service voters** (armed forces in operational areas)
- A nominated proxy votes on behalf of the service voter

### Tender Vote
- If someone has already voted using your identity (**impersonation**), you can still cast a **tender vote**
- The Presiding Officer gives you a **tender ballot paper** (not EVM)
- Your vote is kept separately and counted only if the impersonation is proven

### Challenge Vote
- If a polling agent suspects a voter is impersonating someone, they can **challenge** the voter
- The challenged voter must deposit ₹2 and prove their identity
- If identity is proven, they vote normally and get their ₹2 back

## Voter Turnout
- India's average voter turnout has ranged from **55% to 67%** in general elections
- Highest turnout: 2014 Lok Sabha elections — **66.44%**
- Women voter turnout has steadily increased and nearly matches men in recent elections
- ECI runs **SVEEP (Systematic Voters' Education and Electoral Participation)** to boost turnout

## Voting Rights and Ethics
- Voting is a **right, not a compulsion** (no legal penalty for not voting at the national level)
- It is **illegal** to:
  - Vote more than once
  - Impersonate another voter
  - Bribe or coerce voters
  - Reveal how someone voted (secrecy of ballot)
  - Campaign within 100 meters of a polling station
  - Carry phones or cameras inside the voting compartment

## After Voting
- Your vote is **anonymous** — no one, not even election officials, can trace which button you pressed to your identity
- EVMs are sealed and stored in **strong rooms** after polling closes
- Results are announced on counting day, usually 2-3 days after the last phase of polling
- You can track results live on **results.eci.gov.in**
