# Key Election Legislation in India

## 1. The Constitution of India — Part XV (Articles 324–329)

### Article 324: Election Commission
- Superintendence, direction, and control of elections to Parliament, State Legislatures, and offices of President and Vice-President vested in the Election Commission
- The Election Commission shall consist of the CEC and such number of other ECs as the President may fix

### Article 325: No Person to be Ineligible for Electoral Roll on Grounds of Religion, Race, Caste, or Sex
- There shall be one general electoral roll for every constituency — no separate electorates

### Article 326: Adult Suffrage
- Elections to Lok Sabha and State Assemblies based on adult suffrage — every citizen 18+ has the right to vote (unless disqualified)

### Article 327: Power of Parliament
- Parliament may make laws regarding all matters relating to elections (electoral rolls, delimitation, etc.)

### Article 328: Power of State Legislature
- State Legislature may make provisions for elections to the State Legislature (subject to Article 327)

### Article 329: Bar on Court Interference
- Electoral matters can only be challenged through an **election petition** — not through regular court proceedings

## 2. Representation of the People Act, 1950 (RPA 1950)
**Purpose**: Provides for the preparation and revision of electoral rolls, allocation of seats, and delimitation of constituencies.

### Key Provisions
- **Qualification of voters**: Defines who can be registered as a voter
- **Electoral rolls**: Rules for preparation, revision, and correction of voter lists
- **Allocation of seats**: Provides the basis for distribution of Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha seats among states
- **Delimitation**: Framework for delimitation of constituencies (done by Delimitation Commission)

## 3. Representation of the People Act, 1951 (RPA 1951)
**Purpose**: The primary law governing the conduct of elections in India. Deals with qualifications, disqualifications, corrupt practices, and election offenses.

### Key Provisions

#### Qualifications and Disqualifications
- **Age**: 25+ for Lok Sabha/State Assembly, 30+ for Rajya Sabha/State Council
- **Disqualification** for:
  - Holding an **office of profit** under the government
  - Being of **unsound mind** (declared by court)
  - Being an **undischarged insolvent**
  - Not being a citizen or voluntarily acquiring foreign citizenship
  - **Criminal conviction** with sentence of 2+ years (disqualified for 6 years after release)
  - Conviction for certain offenses: promoting enmity, bribery, undue influence, etc.

#### Corrupt Practices (Section 123)
1. **Bribery**: Offering money/gifts for votes
2. **Undue influence**: Threatening or coercing voters
3. **Appeal to religion, race, caste, community, or language** for votes
4. **Promotion of enmity** between classes
5. **Publication of false statements** about a candidate's personal character
6. **Hiring vehicles** to transport voters (except for disabled voters)
7. **Booth capturing**
8. **Exceeding expenditure limits**

#### Electoral Offenses (Sections 125–136)
- Promoting enmity between classes in connection with elections
- **Bribery** (both giving and receiving)
- **Personation** (voting as someone else)
- **Booth capturing** (seizing a polling station by force)
- Failure to maintain **secrecy of voting**
- Canvassing within **100 meters** of a polling station
- Removal of ballot papers / tampering with EVMs

#### Election Petitions
- Can be filed in the High Court within **45 days** of the result
- Grounds: corrupt practices, non-compliance with law, improper acceptance/rejection of nominations

## 4. Anti-Defection Law — Tenth Schedule (52nd Amendment, 1985)
- A member is **disqualified** from the House if they:
  - Voluntarily give up membership of their political party
  - Vote (or abstain) contrary to the **party whip** without prior permission
- **Exception**: Merger — when two-thirds of a party's legislators merge with another party
- Decision on disqualification: Made by the **Speaker** (Lok Sabha/Assembly) or **Chairman** (Rajya Sabha/Council)
- Amended by **91st Amendment (2003)**: Removed exemption for splits (one-third splitting), only mergers (two-thirds) allowed

## 5. Delimitation Act, 2002
- Provides for the establishment of a **Delimitation Commission** to redraw constituency boundaries
- Based on latest Census data
- Delimitation Commission's orders have the force of law and **cannot be challenged in court**
- Current boundaries based on 2001 Census; **next delimitation expected after 2026 Census**

## 6. The Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952
- Governs the conduct of elections to the offices of President and Vice-President
- Prescribes the procedure for nomination, election, and disputes

## 7. CEC and Other ECs (Appointment, Conditions of Service) Act, 2023
- Replaced the earlier 1991 Act
- CEC and ECs appointed by the President on recommendation of a **Selection Committee**:
  - Prime Minister (Chairperson)
  - Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha
  - A Union Cabinet Minister nominated by PM
- Salary, conditions of service equivalent to that of a **Cabinet Secretary** (changed from Supreme Court Judge equivalent)
- Tenure: 6 years or until age 65, whichever is earlier

## 8. The Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961
- Detailed procedural rules for conduct of elections
- Covers: nomination process, symbols, polling procedure, counting, and results

## 9. Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines) Rules
- Relevant for regulation of social media during elections
- ECI requires social media platforms to take down content violating MCC within specified timelines

## Key Constitutional Amendments Related to Elections

| Amendment | Year | Provision |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| 61st | 1988 | Reduced voting age from 21 to 18 |
| 52nd | 1985 | Added 10th Schedule (Anti-Defection Law) |
| 73rd | 1992 | Panchayati Raj elections (local governance) |
| 74th | 1992 | Municipal elections (urban local bodies) |
| 84th | 2002 | Froze Lok Sabha seats at 543 until post-2026 Census |
| 91st | 2003 | Amended Anti-Defection Law (no splits, only mergers) |
| 104th | 2020 | Removed nominated Anglo-Indian seats in Lok Sabha and State Assemblies |
