# Model Code of Conduct (MCC)

## What is the Model Code of Conduct?
The Model Code of Conduct is a set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission of India (ECI) that governs the behavior of political parties, candidates, and the government during elections. It ensures a **level playing field** and **free and fair elections**.

## When Does It Apply?
- Comes into effect from the **date of election announcement** by the ECI
- Remains in force until the **completion of the election process** (results declared)
- Applies to all parties, candidates, and the ruling government (both central and state)

## Key Provisions

### 1. General Conduct
- No party or candidate shall indulge in any activity that may **aggravate existing differences** or create **mutual hatred** between different castes, communities, or religions
- No **appeals to caste or communal feelings** for securing votes
- Criticism of other parties must be limited to their **policies and programs**, not personal attacks
- No use of mosques, churches, temples, or other places of worship for **election propaganda**
- Voters shall not be **bribed, intimidated, or impersonated**

### 2. Meetings and Processions
- Parties must inform local police and administration about rallies and public meetings in advance
- **No processions** through areas where they may cause disturbance to other communities
- Organizers are responsible for **orderly conduct** at meetings
- Loudspeakers and public address systems must comply with **local noise regulations**

### 3. Polling Day Discipline
- All parties shall cooperate with election officials for **peaceful and orderly polling**
- Only voters with valid IDs and those listed in the electoral roll can enter the polling station
- **No camps** shall be set up by parties within 200 meters of polling stations
- No **identity slips** (in party colors or with party symbols) to be distributed on polling day

### 4. Government and Ruling Party
- **No new projects or schemes** shall be announced that could influence voters
- **No ad-hoc appointments** in government jobs or public works during the election period
- Ministers shall not combine **official visits with electioneering**
- Government transport, machinery, and personnel shall not be used for campaigning
- **No advertisements** at government cost highlighting achievements of the ruling party
- Government accommodation and guest houses shall not be used for campaign purposes
- No **laying of foundation stones** or inauguration of government projects

### 5. Party Manifestos
- Must not contain anything repugnant to the Constitution or **divisive** in nature
- Should not make promises that **vitiate the purity** of the election process
- Parties must explain how proposed programs will be funded

### 6. Media and Social Media
- **Paid news** is prohibited — candidates using media to disguise advertisements as news face action
- Social media posts must not violate the MCC
- **48-hour silence period** before polling — no campaigning, advertisements, or opinion/exit polls
- Political advertisements in electronic media must be **pre-certified** by the Media Certification and Monitoring Committee (MCMC)

## Enforcement

### How is MCC Enforced?
- The MCC is **not legally enforceable** (not backed by specific legislation) — it is a **moral and administrative code**
- However, many MCC provisions overlap with legal provisions in:
  - **Indian Penal Code** (hate speech, bribery)
  - **Representation of the People Act, 1951** (corrupt practices, electoral offenses)
  - **Information Technology Act** (social media violations)
- ECI can:
  - Issue **warnings** and **show-cause notices**
  - Order **FIRs** for legal violations
  - **Ban candidates** from campaigning for specified periods
  - **Defer polling** in extreme cases
  - **De-recognize** a political party for persistent violations

### Monitoring Mechanisms
- **Flying Squads**: Mobile teams that check vehicles, monitor rallies, and seize illegal cash/liquor
- **Static Surveillance Teams**: Posted at checkpoints to intercept movement of cash, liquor, and freebies
- **Video Surveillance**: CCTV at polling stations, videography of rallies and public meetings
- **cVIGIL App**: Any citizen can report MCC violations in real-time by uploading photos/videos; the district authorities must respond within **100 minutes**
- **Social Media Monitoring Cells**: Track online content for violations
- **Expenditure Observers**: Monitor candidate spending

## Common MCC Violations
1. Distribution of cash, liquor, or gifts to voters
2. Communal or hate speeches
3. Misuse of government vehicles or resources
4. Making new policy announcements during MCC period
5. Campaigning during the silence period
6. Defacing public property with posters/banners
7. Exceeding election expenditure limits
8. Paid news or surrogate advertising

## Impact
Despite being a voluntary code, the MCC has been remarkably effective due to:
- Strong institutional reputation of the ECI
- Media scrutiny and public awareness
- Legal backing for many provisions through existing laws
- Precedents of ECI taking action against senior leaders and parties
