# Election Commission of India (ECI)

## Overview
The Election Commission of India is an autonomous constitutional body responsible for administering election processes in India at the national, state, and district levels. It was established on **25 January 1950** (celebrated as National Voters' Day).

## Constitutional Basis
- Established under **Article 324** of the Constitution.
- The President appoints the Chief Election Commissioner (CEC) and other Election Commissioners.
- The CEC can only be removed by Parliament through impeachment (same process as a Supreme Court judge).

## Structure
- **Chief Election Commissioner (CEC)**: Head of the commission
- **Election Commissioners (ECs)**: Currently 2 additional commissioners (multi-member commission since 1993)
- All three members have equal powers; decisions are taken by majority
- Assisted by **Deputy Election Commissioners**, **Secretaries**, and staff at headquarters
- At the state level: **Chief Electoral Officer (CEO)** in each state
- At the district level: **District Election Officer (DEO)**
- At the constituency level: **Returning Officer (RO)**
- At the booth level: **Presiding Officer** and **Polling Officers**

## Key Functions
1. **Conduct of Elections**: Superintendence, direction, and control of all elections to Parliament, State Legislatures, and offices of President and Vice-President.
2. **Electoral Rolls**: Preparation, revision, and maintenance of electoral rolls.
3. **Election Schedule**: Announcement of election dates, phases, and timelines.
4. **Model Code of Conduct (MCC)**: Enforcement of rules that govern the conduct of political parties and candidates during elections.
5. **Recognition of Political Parties**: Granting recognition and allotting symbols to political parties.
6. **Monitoring**: Monitoring election expenditure, use of media, and campaign activities.
7. **Voter Education**: Programs like SVEEP (Systematic Voters' Education and Electoral Participation).
8. **Dispute Resolution**: Resolving disputes related to symbols, party recognition, and election conduct.

## Important Initiatives
- **SVEEP**: Nationwide voter education and awareness program
- **cVIGIL**: Mobile app for citizens to report MCC violations in real-time
- **Voter Helpline 1950**: Toll-free number for voter services
- **NVSP (National Voter Service Portal)**: Online platform for voter registration and services
- **Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT)**: Paper trail system attached to EVMs for verification

## CEC Appointment
- The CEC and ECs are appointed by the President on the advice of a committee comprising the Prime Minister, Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha, and a Union Cabinet Minister nominated by the PM (as per the CEC and Other ECs (Appointment, Conditions of Service) Act, 2023).
- Tenure: 6 years or until age 65, whichever is earlier.
- The CEC enjoys security of tenure and can only be removed through a process similar to removal of a Supreme Court judge.

## Key Statistics
- ECI manages the world's largest electoral exercise
- Over 1 million polling stations in recent general elections
- Approximately 11 million polling and security personnel deployed during general elections
- Election Commission headquarters: Nirvachan Sadan, New Delhi
