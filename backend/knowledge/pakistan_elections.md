# Pakistan Election Process

## Constitutional Framework and System of Government

Pakistan is a federal parliamentary republic governed by the 1973 Constitution. The system follows a Westminster-style parliamentary model with a President as ceremonial head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government. The bicameral Parliament (Majlis-e-Shoora) consists of the National Assembly (336 seats: 266 general, 60 women, 10 minorities) and the Senate (96 seats). Pakistan has four provinces, two autonomous territories, and a federal capital territory.

## Election Administration Body

The Election Commission of Pakistan (ECP) is an independent constitutional body responsible for conducting elections, maintaining electoral rolls, and delimiting constituencies. It is headed by the Chief Election Commissioner and includes four members (one from each province). The ECP supervises all aspects of the electoral process. Website: www.ecp.gov.pk

## Types of Elections

- **General elections**: National Assembly and Provincial Assembly elections (held simultaneously)
- **Senate elections**: Indirect elections by provincial and national legislators
- **Local government elections**: District, tehsil/taluka, and union council levels
- **By-elections**: To fill vacancies in any elected body
- Provincial assemblies: Punjab (371 seats), Sindh (168), Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (145), Balochistan (65)

## Voter Registration Process

All Pakistani citizens aged 18 and older are eligible to vote. The ECP maintains the electoral rolls based on National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA) data. A valid Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC) is required for registration and voting. Electoral rolls are periodically updated and displayed for public claims and objections. Separate voter lists exist for men and women in many constituencies.

## Election Timeline/Calendar

- **Dissolution of assemblies**: Assemblies complete five-year terms or are dissolved earlier
- **Election schedule announced**: Within 48 hours of dissolution
- **Elections must be held**: Within 90 days of dissolution
- **Nomination filing**: 7-10 days after schedule announcement
- **Scrutiny and appeals**: 2-3 weeks
- **Campaign period**: Typically 20-30 days before polling
- **Election day**: As determined by ECP

## Voting Methods and Technology

Pakistan uses paper ballots with official stamps. Voters receive ballots at assigned polling stations, mark them in secret, and deposit them in sealed ballot boxes. Polling stations are separated by gender in many areas. Voter identification is done through CNIC verification. The ECP has piloted biometric verification and electronic voting machines (EVMs) but widespread adoption remains pending. Result Transmission System (RTS) is used for preliminary result communication.

## Campaign Rules and Finance

Political parties must register with the ECP. Campaign spending limits are prescribed by law. Parties must submit annual financial statements and election expense returns. Use of government resources for campaigning is prohibited. The ECP enforces a code of conduct during elections. Campaign activities are restricted during the 24-hour cooling-off period before polling. Media coverage rules apply during the campaign period.

## Counting and Results Process

Ballot boxes are opened and votes counted at polling stations in the presence of polling agents from contesting candidates. Presiding officers compile results on prescribed forms and communicate preliminary results to the Returning Officer. Provisional results are announced by Returning Officers. Final consolidated results are declared by the ECP. Results can be challenged through election tribunals within 45 days.

## Post-Election Procedures

The ECP notifies elected members after final results. The President summons the National Assembly within 21 days. The leader of the majority party or coalition is invited to form the government. The Prime Minister must win a vote of confidence. Election tribunals hear petitions challenging results for up to 120 days (extendable). Post-election audits and reviews are conducted by the ECP.

## Key Legislation

- Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan (1973)
- Elections Act, 2017
- Election Rules, 2017
- Political Parties Order, 2002
- Senate (Election) Act, 1975
- Delimitation of Constituencies Act, 1974
- ECP Code of Conduct for Political Parties and Candidates
