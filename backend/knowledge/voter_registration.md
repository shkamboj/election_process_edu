# Voter Registration in India

## Eligibility
To register as a voter in India, you must:
1. Be a **citizen of India**
2. Be **18 years of age or older** on the qualifying date (1st January of the year of revision of electoral roll)
3. Be a **resident** of the constituency where you wish to register
4. Not be disqualified under any law (e.g., unsound mind, corrupt practices, certain criminal convictions)

## How to Register

### Online Registration (Recommended)
1. Visit the **National Voter Service Portal (NVSP)**: https://voters.eci.gov.in
2. Click on "New Voter Registration" (Form 6)
3. Fill in personal details: Name, Date of Birth, Address, Relation (Father/Mother/Husband)
4. Upload documents:
   - **Age proof**: Birth certificate, school leaving certificate, passport, PAN card, or Aadhaar
   - **Address proof**: Aadhaar, utility bill, bank passbook, ration card, passport, or rent agreement
   - **Passport-size photograph**
5. Submit the form
6. Your application will be verified by the local Electoral Registration Officer (ERO)
7. A **Booth Level Officer (BLO)** may visit your residence for physical verification
8. Once approved, your name is added to the electoral roll and you receive your **EPIC (Elector's Photo Identity Card)** / Voter ID

### Offline Registration
1. Obtain **Form 6** from the local ERO office or download from ECI website
2. Fill in the form and attach required documents
3. Submit to the ERO of your constituency
4. Verification process is the same as online

### Through Voter Helpline App
1. Download the **Voter Helpline** app (available on Android and iOS)
2. Register and fill in Form 6 through the app
3. Upload documents and submit

## Important Forms
| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Form 6** | New voter registration (Indian residents) |
| **Form 6A** | Registration for overseas/NRI voters |
| **Form 6B** | Aadhaar collection for existing voters |
| **Form 7** | Objection to inclusion of a name in the electoral roll |
| **Form 8** | Correction of entries in the electoral roll (name, address, photo, etc.) |
| **Form 8A** | Transposition of entry (shifting within the same constituency) |

## EPIC (Voter ID Card)
- **Full Name**: Elector's Photo Identity Card
- Contains: Voter's name, photo, father's/husband's name, date of birth, gender, address, EPIC number, constituency details
- **Not mandatory** for voting — you can use 12 alternative photo IDs (Aadhaar, passport, driving license, PAN card, etc.)
- Can be downloaded as **e-EPIC** (digital version) from the NVSP portal or Voter Helpline app

## Electoral Roll
- The electoral roll is the official list of all registered voters in a constituency
- Prepared and revised by the ERO under the supervision of ECI
- **Summary revision**: Annual update (additions, deletions, corrections), usually with 1st January as the qualifying date
- **Special revision**: Ordered by ECI before a specific election
- Voters can check their name in the electoral roll on the NVSP portal or via SMS

## NRI/Overseas Voter Registration
- Indian citizens living abroad can register as **overseas electors** using **Form 6A**
- Must provide passport details
- As of now, overseas voters must be **physically present** at the polling booth in their constituency to vote (no postal or e-voting yet, though proposed)
- The Representation of the People (Amendment) Act, 2010 enabled NRI registration

## Voter Helpline
- **Toll-free number**: 1950
- Available for queries about registration, polling station location, EPIC status, and election-related complaints
- Also accessible via the Voter Helpline mobile app

## Tips for Voters
1. **Check your name** in the electoral roll well before elections
2. **Update your address** if you've moved — use Form 8A (same constituency) or Form 6 (new constituency)
3. **Carry a valid photo ID** on polling day
4. **Know your polling booth** — check on NVSP or Voter Helpline app
5. **Register early** — don't wait until elections are announced, as registration closes during the election period
