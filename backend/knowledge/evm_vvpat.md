# EVM and VVPAT — How India Votes

## Electronic Voting Machine (EVM)

### What is an EVM?
An EVM is an electronic device used for recording votes in Indian elections. India was the **first country** to use EVMs nationwide (fully adopted in 2004 general elections, pilot use since 1982 in Kerala).

### Components
An EVM has two units:
1. **Control Unit (CU)**: Operated by the Presiding Officer. It controls the voting process, stores vote counts, and is kept on the officer's table.
2. **Ballot Unit (BU)**: Placed inside the voting compartment. It has buttons next to candidate names and party symbols. The voter presses the button of their choice.

### How It Works
1. The Presiding Officer presses the **ballot button** on the Control Unit to enable one vote
2. A light and beep on the Ballot Unit indicate the machine is ready
3. The voter presses the button next to their chosen candidate
4. A light glows next to the selected candidate and a long beep confirms the vote
5. The machine locks — no second vote can be cast until the officer enables the next one
6. The vote is stored in the Control Unit's memory

### Key Features
- **Stand-alone device**: No internet, Wi-Fi, or Bluetooth connectivity — cannot be hacked remotely
- **Battery operated**: Uses a 7.5V alkaline battery — works without electricity
- **One-time programmable chip**: Software is burned into the chip during manufacturing and cannot be altered
- **Capacity**: Each Ballot Unit can hold 16 candidates; up to 4 BUs can be connected to one CU (64 candidates max)
- **Manufactured by**: Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL) and Electronics Corporation of India Limited (ECIL) — both government companies
- **Tamper-detection**: Any attempt to open the machine triggers visible seal breakage

### M3 EVM (Latest Generation)
- Introduced in 2013, used in all elections since
- Features:
  - Self-diagnostics at each key press
  - Digital signature for each vote
  - Real-time clock for timestamping
  - Encrypted data storage
  - Tamper-resistant design with multiple seals

## VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail)

### What is VVPAT?
VVPAT is a printer-like device attached to the EVM that provides a **paper receipt** of the vote cast, allowing the voter to verify their vote.

### How It Works
1. Voter presses a button on the Ballot Unit
2. The VVPAT printer prints a slip showing the **candidate's name, serial number, and party symbol**
3. The slip is displayed behind a **transparent window** for **7 seconds**
4. The slip then drops into a **sealed box** (voter cannot take it)
5. The voter can verify that the correct candidate was recorded

### Purpose
- Provides an **independent paper trail** to verify EVM accuracy
- Acts as a **backup** for auditing election results
- Increases voter confidence in the electronic voting process

### Legal Mandate
- Supreme Court ordered VVPAT deployment in **Subramanian Swamy v. ECI (2013)**
- Used in all polling stations nationwide since the **2019 general elections**

### VVPAT Verification During Counting
- **5 randomly selected EVMs** per Assembly constituency are verified by matching VVPAT paper slips with EVM electronic count
- This was mandated by the Supreme Court in **2019**
- If there's a discrepancy, the VVPAT paper count is considered final

## Polling Day Procedure (Step by Step)

### For the Voter
1. **Arrive** at your designated polling station
2. **Queue up** — separate lines may exist for men, women, and persons with disabilities
3. **Show your ID** — EPIC or any of the 12 approved photo IDs (Aadhaar, passport, DL, PAN card, etc.)
4. **Verification** — polling officer checks your name against the electoral roll
5. **Indelible ink** — your left index finger is marked with indelible ink (prevents double voting; lasts ~4 weeks)
6. **Receive voter slip** — a paper slip with your serial number
7. **Enter the voting compartment** — voting is done in a private screened area
8. **Cast your vote** — press the button next to your chosen candidate (or NOTA) on the Ballot Unit
9. **Verify on VVPAT** — check the paper slip in the VVPAT window (7 seconds)
10. **Exit** the polling station

### For Persons with Disabilities
- **Accessible polling stations** with ramps and assistance
- **Braille labels** on EVMs for visually impaired voters
- A **companion** can accompany voters who need physical assistance
- **Postal ballot** facility for certain categories of disabled voters

### Postal Ballots
Available for:
- **Service voters** (armed forces, paramilitary)
- **Government employees on election duty**
- **Voters above 80 years of age** (optional)
- **Persons with disabilities** (optional, PwD with 40%+ disability)
- **COVID-positive voters** (introduced in 2020)
- **Essential service workers** on polling day

## Common Questions

**Q: Can EVMs be hacked?**
A: EVMs are stand-alone devices with no network connectivity. The software is on a one-time programmable chip that cannot be re-written. Multiple layers of administrative and technical safeguards exist. No proven case of EVM tampering has been established in any Indian court.

**Q: What if VVPAT shows wrong candidate?**
A: The voter can report to the Presiding Officer. A test vote is conducted in the presence of the voter and candidates' agents. If the machine is faulty, it is replaced.

**Q: What if the EVM malfunctions?**
A: The Presiding Officer replaces it with a reserve EVM. Votes already cast on the malfunctioned machine are counted separately.

**Q: Is voting compulsory in India?**
A: No. Voting is a **right, not a legal obligation** at the national level. However, some states (like Gujarat) have local laws making voting mandatory for local body elections.
