# Indonesia Election Process

## Constitutional Framework and System of Government

Indonesia is a unitary presidential constitutional republic. The 1945 Constitution (UUD 1945), amended four times between 1999 and 2002, establishes a presidential system with separation of powers. The President serves as head of state and head of government for a five-year term, limited to two consecutive terms. Indonesia has a bicameral legislature: the People's Representative Council (DPR) with 580 seats and the Regional Representative Council (DPD) with 136 seats, together forming the People's Consultative Assembly (MPR).

## Election Administration Body

The General Elections Commission (Komisi Pemilihan Umum/KPU) is the independent body responsible for organizing and administering elections at national and regional levels. The Election Supervisory Board (Bawaslu) oversees election conduct and handles disputes. The Honorary Council of Election Organizers (DKPP) handles ethics violations by election officials. Website: www.kpu.go.id

## Types of Elections

- **Presidential election**: Direct popular vote with a majority requirement (50%+1 or plurality with geographic spread)
- **Legislative elections**: DPR (national parliament), DPD (regional council), provincial DPRD, and district/city DPRD
- **Regional head elections (Pilkada)**: Direct election of governors, mayors, and regents
- Since 2019, presidential and legislative elections are held simultaneously

## Voter Registration Process

All Indonesian citizens aged 17 or older (or married) are eligible to vote. The KPU conducts voter registration using population data from the Ministry of Home Affairs. A preliminary voter list (DPS) is published for public review before finalization into the permanent voter list (DPT). Voters receive a voter notification card (C6). Citizens can register at local KPU offices if not on the list.

## Election Timeline/Calendar

- **Voter list preparation**: 12-14 months before election day
- **Candidate registration**: 8-9 months before election
- **Campaign period**: Approximately 75 days before election
- **Quiet period**: 3 days before voting
- **Election day**: Typically held in February or April every five years
- **Results announcement**: Within 35 days of voting

## Voting Methods and Technology

Indonesia uses paper ballots marked by punching a hole (coblos) through the candidate's name or party symbol using a nail. Voting takes place at local polling stations (TPS), each serving approximately 300 voters. Vote counting is conducted publicly at each TPS immediately after polls close. Results are tabulated manually at progressively higher levels (village, district, province, national). Real-count systems provide digital tallying for transparency.

## Campaign Rules and Finance

Political parties must meet a parliamentary threshold (4%) to gain DPR seats. Campaign spending limits are set by the KPU. Campaigns may use mass media, rallies, and social media within regulated periods. Campaign finance reports must be submitted to the KPU and audited by public accountants. Government resources cannot be used for campaigning. A three-day cooling-off period precedes election day.

## Counting and Results Process

Votes are counted publicly at each polling station by election officials in the presence of party witnesses and observers. Results are recorded on official tally forms (C1) and progressively recapitulated at district, regency/city, provincial, and national levels. The KPU announces official results within 35 days. Quick count results from survey institutions provide early unofficial projections.

## Post-Election Procedures

Disputes over presidential election results are adjudicated by the Constitutional Court (Mahkamah Konstitusi) within 14 days. Legislative election disputes follow a similar process. Elected officials are inaugurated according to constitutional timelines. The President is inaugurated in October. Post-election evaluations are conducted by the KPU and Bawaslu.

## Key Legislation

- 1945 Constitution (UUD 1945) as amended
- Law No. 7 of 2017 on General Elections
- Law No. 10 of 2016 on Regional Head Elections
- Law No. 2 of 2011 on Political Parties
- KPU Regulations (PKPU) issued for each election cycle
