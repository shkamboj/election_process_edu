# Brazil Election Process

## Constitutional Framework and System of Government

Brazil is a federal presidential constitutional republic governed by the 1988 Constitution. The system features separation of powers among the executive (President), legislative (National Congress), and judiciary. The President serves as head of state and government for a four-year term, renewable once. The National Congress is bicameral: the Chamber of Deputies (513 seats) and the Federal Senate (81 seats, 3 per state). Brazil has 26 states and one Federal District.

## Election Administration Body

The Superior Electoral Court (Tribunal Superior Eleitoral/TSE) oversees all elections in Brazil. It is the highest authority on electoral matters and manages voter registration, party registration, campaign oversight, and vote counting. Regional Electoral Courts (TREs) operate in each state. Electoral judges and boards administer local polling. Website: www.tse.jus.br

## Types of Elections

- **Presidential election**: Direct popular vote with two-round system (runoff if no candidate wins 50%+1)
- **Gubernatorial elections**: Direct election of state governors (also two-round)
- **Legislative elections**: Federal deputies (proportional representation), senators (first-past-the-post)
- **Municipal elections**: Mayors and city council members (held two years after general elections)
- General elections occur every four years; municipal elections occur in intervening even years

## Voter Registration Process

Voting is compulsory for literate citizens aged 18 to 70. It is optional for those aged 16-17, over 70, and illiterate citizens. Registration is done at local electoral offices (cartórios eleitorais) or online. Each voter receives a voter registration card (título de eleitor). Biometric data collection has been progressively expanded. Non-voters face restrictions on obtaining passports and public employment if they fail to justify absence.

## Election Timeline/Calendar

- **Party conventions and candidacy registration**: June–August of election year
- **Campaign period**: August 16 to election eve
- **First round**: First Sunday of October
- **Second round (if needed)**: Last Sunday of October
- **Inauguration**: January 1 of the following year (President, governors, and legislators)

## Voting Methods and Technology

Brazil pioneered electronic voting with the introduction of electronic voting machines (urnas eletrônicas) in 1996, achieving full adoption by 2000. These standalone machines are not connected to the internet. Voters enter candidate numbers on a keypad and confirm their choice on a screen. Each machine prints a bulletin of results (boletim de urna) immediately after polls close. Since 2022, a printed vote verification system has been debated and partially piloted.

## Campaign Rules and Finance

Corporate donations to candidates are banned since 2015 (Supreme Court ruling). Individual donations are capped at 10% of the donor's gross income from the previous year. Public campaign funds (Fundo Eleitoral) are distributed among parties. Free airtime on radio and television (horário eleitoral gratuito) is allocated proportionally. Campaign periods and spending limits are set by the TSE. Online campaigning and social media are regulated.

## Counting and Results Process

Electronic voting machines calculate results instantly at each polling station. Once polls close, each machine prints a public results bulletin. Results are transmitted electronically to the TRE and TSE. Official results are typically announced on the same night as the election. The TSE consolidates national results. The entire process from voting to result announcement usually takes only a few hours.

## Post-Election Procedures

Elected officials are proclaimed by the electoral courts. Challenges to election results can be filed with the TSE within three days. The electoral courts can annul elections in cases of proven fraud or abuse of economic or political power. Transition teams are established for incoming governments. The President takes office on January 1.

## Key Legislation

- Constitution of the Federative Republic of Brazil (1988)
- Electoral Code (Law No. 4,737 of 1965)
- Law of Elections (Law No. 9,504 of 1997)
- Law of Political Parties (Law No. 9,096 of 1995)
- Complementary Law No. 64 of 1990 (Ineligibility Law/Lei da Ficha Limpa as amended)
- TSE Resolutions issued for each election
