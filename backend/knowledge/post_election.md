# Post-Election Process

## Counting Process

### Day of Counting
- Counting happens at **designated counting centers**, usually 2-3 days after the last phase of polling
- Begins at **8:00 AM** under the supervision of the Returning Officer (RO)
- Candidates and their counting agents are present

### Counting Procedure
1. **Postal ballots counted first**
   - Opened in the presence of candidates/agents
   - Scrutinized for validity (signatures, seals)
   - Valid postal ballots are counted and tallied

2. **EVM counting (round by round)**
   - EVMs are brought from strong rooms to counting halls in batches
   - Seals are verified before opening
   - The Presiding Officer's diary and account of votes are checked
   - **Result button** on the Control Unit is pressed to display vote counts
   - Counts are recorded on result sheets
   - Each batch of EVMs constitutes one **round**

3. **VVPAT Verification**
   - Paper slips from **5 randomly selected EVMs** per Assembly segment are counted
   - VVPAT count is matched with EVM electronic count
   - If discrepancy: VVPAT paper count prevails

4. **Tallying**
   - All rounds are compiled
   - Postal + EVM counts combined
   - Candidate with the **highest total votes** is declared the winner

### Trends and Live Results
- ECI updates results on **results.eci.gov.in** in real-time
- Media channels display trends as each round is counted
- Trends can shift as different areas within a constituency are counted

## Election Petitions and Disputes

### Filing an Election Petition
- A defeated candidate or any elector can challenge the result by filing an **election petition**
- Must be filed in the **High Court** of the state where the constituency is located
- Time limit: **45 days** from the date of election result
- Grounds for challenging:
  - **Corrupt practices** by the winning candidate (bribery, undue influence, appeal to religion/caste)
  - **Improper acceptance or rejection** of nominations
  - **Non-compliance** with constitutional or legal provisions
  - **Booth capturing** or rigging

### Court's Powers
- The High Court can:
  - **Declare the election void** (fresh election ordered)
  - **Declare the petitioner** as the rightful winner (if they prove they had more valid votes)
  - **Dismiss** the petition
- Appeal lies to the **Supreme Court**

## Recounting
- A candidate can request a **recount** if the margin of victory is narrow or they suspect errors
- The RO may order a recount of specific EVMs or VVPAT slips
- Requests must be made **at the counting center** before the result is declared
- A recount of VVPAT paper slips can be ordered by the RO or by a court order

## Security Deposit Forfeiture
- A candidate loses their security deposit if they fail to secure **one-sixth (1/6th)** of total valid votes in the constituency
- This provision discourages frivolous candidates

## Government Formation

### At the Central Level
1. After results are declared, the **President** assesses which party/coalition commands a majority in Lok Sabha
2. The President invites the leader of the majority party/coalition to form the government
3. The **Prime Minister** is sworn in by the President
4. The PM recommends a **Council of Ministers**, who are also sworn in
5. The new government must prove its majority on the **floor of the house** through a vote of confidence (if required)

### At the State Level
1. The **Governor** invites the majority party/coalition leader to form the government
2. The **Chief Minister** is sworn in by the Governor
3. The CM recommends ministers, sworn in by the Governor
4. Floor test if required

### Coalition Politics
- When no single party wins a majority, parties form **pre-election** or **post-election alliances**
- The alliance that can demonstrate majority support (272+ seats in Lok Sabha) forms the government
- A **Common Minimum Programme (CMP)** is often agreed upon by coalition partners
- The Governor/President plays a key role in determining who gets the first chance

### Hung Parliament/Assembly
- When no party or coalition has a clear majority
- The President/Governor may:
  - Invite the **single largest party** to form government and prove majority
  - Invite a **coalition** that claims majority support
  - In rare cases, recommend **President's Rule** (for states) if no government can be formed

## Transition of Power
- The outgoing PM/CM and Council of Ministers continue as **caretaker government** until the new government is sworn in
- Key files and briefings are handed over
- The new government begins its term with the first session of the newly elected house

## Post-Election Analysis
- **ECI publishes** detailed statistics: voter turnout (constituency-wise, phase-wise), gender-wise participation, NOTA votes
- **Political parties** analyze results to strategize for future elections
- **Media and analysts** study voting patterns, swing factors, and demographic trends
